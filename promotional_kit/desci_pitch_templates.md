> **Hello everyone,**
> 
> We have just released **OpenSMA**, an open-source (MIT licensed) end-to-end computational drug discovery platform for Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMA). 
> 
> The platform automates:
> -   **De novo small molecule generation** (Genetic Algorithm with RDKit, optimized for CNS MPO & QED)
> -   **ASO design** (Freier 1986 Nearest-Neighbor RNA-RNA thermodynamics)
> -   **3D molecular docking & Metropolis Monte Carlo pocket dynamics** at 310 K (simulating target-complex thermal stability)
> -   **5-Compartment PBPK ODE systems** calibrated against Phase 3 clinical data (Finkel 2017 & Baranello 2021)
> -   **Monte Carlo clinical cohort simulation** (10,000 virtual patients) for a multi-modal "Full Cure" combination therapy (ASO bridge + CRISPR Base Editing).
> 
> We have also written a highly detailed **wet-lab experimental protocol** (transfection in GM03813 SMA fibroblasts, RT-qPCR splicing assays with Exon 6/8 primers, Western Blotting) to bridge the dry-lab findings with biological validation.
> 
> We are actively seeking **experimental biology partners** within the DeSci space to help validate our top-ranked candidates in-vitro. If your lab or DAO is interested in open-source drug discovery for rare diseases, we'd love to chat!

## 2. ResearchHub Gönderi/Yayın Metni
ResearchHub platformuna projenin `OpenSMA_Academic_Monograph.md` veya `OpenSMA_Master_Technical_Report.md` dosyasını "Post/Document" olarak yüklerken kullanabileceğiniz özet tanıtım yazısıdır.

**Başlık:** OpenSMA: An Open-Source, End-to-End Computational Pipeline and Systems Biology Simulator for SMA Therapeutics

**Özet / Açıklama:**
> In this document, we present **OpenSMA**, a fully integrated dry-lab and wet-lab framework designed to accelerate drug discovery for Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMA). 
> 
> By compiling classical biophysics equations (Freier nearest-neighbor doublet thermodynamics, Metropolis-Hastings 3D pocket stability) and clinical pharmacology models (5-compartment PBPK differential equations), the platform screens de novo small molecules, CRISPR base editing guides, and antisense oligonucleotides (ASOs).
> 
> **Why it matters:**
> Conventional drug discovery pipelines are proprietary and prohibitively expensive, which is especially detrimental for rare neuromuscular diseases like SMA. OpenSMA democratizes this workflow by providing:
> 1.  Open-source software executing all 12 discovery and simulation phases.
> 2.  Literature-calibrated PBPK models predicting 5-year patient outcomes (survival, motor milestone gains).
> 3.  A complete wet-lab validation blueprint, enabling researchers globally to synthesize and test candidates in-vitro with standard lab equipment.
> 
> We publish this master technical dossier to invite open peer review, academic collaboration, and community-driven verification of our computational predictions.
